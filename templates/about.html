The laboratory environment monitoring and alert system (LEMAS) is a system comprised of several Raspberry Pi devices, connected to instruments, and reporting whether the environment is within desirable conditions, or undesirable which it sends e-mails/text messages to alert users of the condition. The site is a means to remotely visualize data from these devices, and is often called the "Master LEMAS".

The data displayed does not automatically update in your browser. You must manually refresh the page to see new data. This website rebuilds itself every 300 seconds.

This gift of data is brought to you and maintained by Michael Braine (michael.braine@nist.gov), Dimensional Metrology Group 685.10, PML. January 2020

The NIST distribution of LEMAS source, including sensor manuals and installation instructions, is available on NIST's internal gitlab server: https://gitlab.nist.gov/gitlab/braine/lemasdist.

The source for the website construction and many-environment analysis system is available on NIST's internal gitlab: https://gitlab.nist.gov/gitlab/braine/LEMASmaster.
